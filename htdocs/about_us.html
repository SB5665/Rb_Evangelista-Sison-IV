■What are vtubers?

Vtubers, or virtual YouTubers, are a type of online entertainment in which people or groups create videos in which they represent themselves using 3D avatars or 2D characters. These avatars or characters are frequently animated and can interact with physical objects or surroundings. Vtubers often produce a wide range of content, including livestreams, music videos, gaming videos, and vlogs. The content is frequently provided in a lighthearted or comic tone, and may contain aspects such as skits, parodies, and product reviews. Many Vtubers are known for their distinct personalities and characters, and they frequently connect with their fans on social media platforms such as Twitter and Instagram. Some Vtubers also sell products such as T-shirts and action figurines based on their avatars or characters. Vtubers are not a new concept, but their popularity has grown in recent years, notably in Japan, where several Vtubers have become quite popular and have even gone on to participate in live performances and events. Some Vtubers have a large following on YouTube as well as other streaming platforms such as TikTok, Twitch, and others.

Examples of these vtuber groups are Hololive, Nijisanji, Vshojo, VOMS, PRISM Project, and many more.
